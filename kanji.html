Let's learn some 漢字!

A little background information:


Prior to the advent of the Japanese language, there was no formal writing system; meaning that communication was dependent on regional dialects.
The Kanji were imported from China in the 7th century, paving the way towards greater literacy and communication within Japanese society.
As time progressed Japan began to severe its ties with China; through the introduction of the Kana in replace of Kanji. Although that didn't happen...
Fast forward into present day and the Kanji still remains an integeral part of the Japanese language, providing gramatical repreive at the cost of complexity.
Each Kanji has two unique readings and pronounciation which can interchange at any moment within a sentence; the Onyomi and Kunyomi.

What seperates the Onyomi and the Kunyomi?

Onyomi readings tend to have more similarities to their Chinese counterparts and are mostly used as nouns.
Kunyomi readings are closer in similarity to traditional Japanese pronounciation and are frequently used as adjectives and verbs.
